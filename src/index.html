Indigenous children are far more likely to be taken from their homes

  • The number of indigenous children in government care has barely changed in 20 years, even as the number of non-indigenous children in care has plunged.

  • Authorities are nearly twenty times more likely to take an Indigenous child from their home than they are to take other children.

  • On top of that, the province spends far less onindigenous children in care than they do on non-indigenous children.
